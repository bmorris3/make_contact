Frequently Asked Questions

When is it more effective to call/email/mail and why?

We recommend that you show up to town halls or call if the matter in hand is urgent or if your message is short. For a more detailed message, we recommend sending emails or mails. While the Congress staffers may send you generic letters in response to your email or mail, they have to interact with you directly if you call or show up at a town hall. Thus to spur a more rapid change, phone call or town hall attendance are helpful.

Why do we insist on personalized letter?

Generic letters tend to generate generic responses. If you take your time to produce a personalized letter with facts and figures, Congress staffers are more likely to consider your letter seriously and send you a personalized response.

Why is it important to ask for a specific response from your representative?

If you do not ask for specific responses, the office of the representative may send you a generic response. When you ask for specific responses, the office will have to send you a personalized response and you will know more about whether your representative supports your cause or is against it.

Why should you focus on contacting your own representatives?

Because your vote directly influences the status of your representative, it is more likely that your congressional representative will take your letter more seriously than a letter from someone whom he or she does not represent. Thus we strongly recommend that you include your address or use a phone number with your home address area code to have more impact.

When is it effective to contact members of specific committees?

It is best to contact members when laws are being drafted or are being debated in the Congress.
